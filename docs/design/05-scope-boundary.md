# 05 — Scope boundary (BBNJ)

Grow **instrument by instrument**, not by sweep. Breadth is worthless if any record's provenance
is doubtful.

**In scope**
- The BBNJ Agreement (2023) — the core instrument, all authentic languages.
- UNCLOS (1982) — the parent convention; the provisions directly relevant to BBNJ
  (e.g. the Part XV dispute-settlement machinery replicated by Art. 60; Art. 287/298).
- The two prior UNCLOS implementing agreements — for context/cross-reference.
- Preparatory Commission outputs (established by UNGA res. 78/272) and, once it meets,
  Conference of the Parties (COP) decisions, rules of procedure and subsidiary-body instruments.
- Clearing-House Mechanism **governing rules** (distinguish the governing *law* from operational
  *data* it hosts).
- National and regional (e.g. EU) implementing measures as they are enacted.

**Out of scope (until a dated decision)**
- The deep-seabed-mining regime (ISA / UNCLOS Part XI + 1994 Agreement) — a **separate** corpus.
- General law of the sea beyond the BBNJ-relevant provisions.
- Fisheries-management regimes (RFMOs, UN Fish Stocks Agreement) except where directly cross-referenced.
- Commentary, policy and advocacy that is not law (reference-only, derived layer).

**Borderline items** go to `queue/candidates.md` for a recorded decision — not straight into the corpus.
